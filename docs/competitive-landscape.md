# Competitive Landscape

What we know about competitors as of mid-2026. Updated quarterly. Every entry includes: position, why they're not us, what we learn from them.

## Direct EAC players

### Esheria (Kenya)
- **Position:** AI-powered legal research and drafting assistant for Kenyan lawyers. Founded by ex-Kenyan-Bar tech-aligned founders. Focused on research-and-drafting workflow.
- **Differentiation from Parasol:** Esheria targets law firms and the research-and-drafting workflow (legal Q&A, drafting from precedent). Parasol targets in-house counsel and the contract-triage workflow (review inbound contract against playbook, return redline). Different buyer, different product, different price model.
- **Risk to Parasol:** They are the closest in geography and mindshare. If they pivot to in-house contract review, the gap closes.
- **What we learn:** Their corpus quality is good; their willingness to invest in Kenyan-specific content is the right play. Their UX is research-app-shaped (chat + sidebar), not workflow-shaped.

### Wakili (Kenya)
- **Position:** Consumer access-to-justice platform. Find a lawyer, ask basic legal questions, simple document templates.
- **Differentiation:** Different category. B2C, low-ticket, mass market. Not a competitor to Parasol's mid-corporate B2B.
- **Risk:** Negligible.
- **What we learn:** Consumer access-to-justice is a genuine separate market with its own dynamics; staying disciplined on B2B is the right call.

### Sheria Plex (Kenya)
- **Position:** Kenyan legal information portal. Statutes, cases, basic Q&A.
- **Differentiation:** Information, not workflow. Not transactional, not contract-review.
- **Risk:** Low. They could expand but their structure is content-portal not SaaS-product.

## Pan-African / regional

### Modulaw (Nigeria, expanding regionally)
- **Position:** AI legal assistant for African law firms. Drafting, research, document review. Backed by African-focused VC.
- **Differentiation from Parasol:** Law-firm-side product. Sells to firms, not in-house. Pan-African scope means Kenya is one of many; they cannot localise as deeply as Parasol per-jurisdiction.
- **Risk to Parasol:** Real. They will eventually have a Kenya offering. Their distribution is firms, who refer to in-house clients.
- **What we learn:** Pan-African scope limits per-jurisdiction depth. Our window is the 18-24 months before they get a Kenyan playbook authored to depth.

### Afriwise (Belgium-headquartered, pan-African coverage)
- **Position:** Compliance intelligence platform. Regulatory monitoring across African jurisdictions for multinational corporates.
- **Differentiation:** Not contract review. Compliance information layer; reads regulatory updates, alerts on relevance, structures ongoing compliance programs. Enterprise-priced (USD 30-100k/year).
- **Risk to Parasol:** Indirect. They're the deep enterprise compliance buyer. They would partner before competing.
- **What we learn:** Mid-corporates can't afford Afriwise; the gap between "research portal" (Esheria) and "enterprise compliance intelligence" (Afriwise) is exactly Parasol's lane.

## Global players

### Robin AI (UK, collapsed late 2025)
- **Position:** Was the leading "AI contract review" product. Built managed services + SaaS hybrid. Raised >$50M. Wound down operations Q4 2025 after failing to scale.
- **What happened:** Managed services (employed lawyers + AI-assisted review for client contracts) compressed gross margin while competing with the AI it depended on. Customer growth required hiring; AI quality didn't reduce per-customer headcount fast enough; pricing pressure from Harvey/Legora cut into the SaaS-only segment.
- **What we learn:** The managed-services arm is what killed it. Pure SaaS with pure software economics is the durable shape. CLAUDE.md mandates: no managed services. Refer escalations; do not employ lawyers.

### Harvey (US, $5B+ valuation)
- **Position:** Frontier-level AI legal copilot. Originally law-firm-side (Magic Circle, AmLaw 100). Expanding to Big-4 audit and large in-house. US-and-EU-centric corpus and playbooks.
- **Differentiation from Parasol:** Different ICP (large firm, Fortune 500 in-house). Different price point (USD 100-500k/year). Different geography (no EAC localisation, no Kenya corpus, no Swahili).
- **Risk to Parasol:** Will not price down to EAC SME for years. Eventual risk only if a multinational subsidiary in Nairobi has Harvey deployed at HQ; Parasol's workspace adoption flow absorbs those organically.
- **What we learn:** Their UX bar is high — Parasol must match or exceed for the segment we serve, or "global" players will look more polished even in our home market. Their model-routing strategy (multiple frontier models per task) is industry-standard and validates ours.

### Legora (Sweden, ~$1B valuation)
- **Position:** Same category as Harvey — frontier AI for sophisticated legal teams. Slightly more European, slightly more in-house focused.
- **Differentiation:** Same as Harvey for our purposes. EU-focused, no EAC localisation.
- **Risk:** Same as Harvey. Premium tier above Parasol's market.
- **What we learn:** Their visual design and product polish are the bar we measure ourselves against (BRAND.md draws from this). Their go-to-market motion (mid-market in-house teams, founder-led trials) maps closely to ours; the playbook is well-validated.

### LawGeex, Spellbook, Genie AI, others
- Various mid-tier global players in contract review. All US/EU-centric. None localised for EAC. None a Kenya-procurement-team-realistic option for cross-border data.
- **Risk:** None directly. Aggregate risk is buyer confusion ("isn't there already an AI for this?") which is solved by clear positioning, not by feature parity.

## Who Parasol's customers consider when evaluating

Based on early discovery conversations:

1. **Doing nothing** (status quo of external counsel + queue) — this is the actual main competitor
2. **Esheria** — for some buyers, framed as "legal AI in Kenya"
3. **Spreadsheet + Word + a junior associate's time** — equivalent functional outcome, much higher cost
4. **Existing CLM (Ironclad, ContractPodAi)** — usually not a real option at the price point but mentioned
5. **External counsel** — both as alternative and as the surface Parasol-will-replace

Notably absent from real consideration: Harvey, Legora, Robin AI (collapsed), LawGeex.

## Strategic implications

- The window for EAC localisation is open. Robin AI's collapse + Harvey/Legora's enterprise focus + Modulaw's pan-African breadth-not-depth = 18-24 month period before serious competition arrives.
- Trust is the moat, then corpus depth, then playbook quality, then UX. In that order.
- The single biggest competitive threat is *not* another AI legal product. It's a procurement officer at a Kenyan bank deciding "we'll wait for Harvey to get to Africa." Parasol's narrative must counter that directly: Harvey is enterprise, won't price down, won't localise.
- Esheria pivot risk is real but slow. Their VC funding signals research-and-drafting commitment.
